Germany's Run to World Cup Victory

by LUCY MAYNARD
The German men's national soccer team did not lose a single game on the road to winning the 2014 World Cup, their fourth title. This story -- told through graphics -- documents their path to victory.

Germany Got Better as Tournament Progressed

Germany tied only one game en route to winning the World Cup -- a surprise result against Ghana -- but they did face several scares. They required extra-time to beat Algeria in the quarter-finals and took 120 minutes to defeat Argentina in the final.

Stadiums Hosting Wins

Germany played seven games in six different stadiums en route to winning their fourth World Cup in 2014, traveling hundreds of miles across Brazil. They won six games and drew one, 2-2 against Ghana on June 21.

How Germany Won

Germany won the World Cup by controlling the ball with more crisp passes than any other team in the tournament, taking more high percentage shots and scoring buckets of goals while keeping their opponents from finding the back of the net.

Germany retained possession of the ball better than any other team. Germany averaged 726 passes per game, more than any other team.

Germany had the second highest percentage of passes complete-tied with Spain with 82 percent and behind Italy with 85 percent. But Germany attempted and completed more than twice the number of passes as Spain and Italy.

Germany made more attempts on target per game than any other team. Nearly three out of four-72 percent-of its shots was on target, a higher rate than any other team.

Germany scored more goals per game than any other team in the tournament. Germany also gave up fewer goals per game than any other team.

Germany's goalie had more total saves than any other goalie except USA's. And the USA didn't even make it past the round of 16.

Germany had better control of the ball. Germany scored more open play goals per game than any other team.

A Passing Machine

Germany moved the ball around the field at a rapid clip throughout the tournament, passing the ball an average of 726 times per game -- well above the median of 513 passes for all 32 teams.

Impressive on Offense, Stingy on Defense

It may seem obvious, but one reason Germany won the World Cup is that it scored more goals per game, on average, than any other team in the tournament, while letting in fewer goals per game on average than all but one team, Costa Rica.
